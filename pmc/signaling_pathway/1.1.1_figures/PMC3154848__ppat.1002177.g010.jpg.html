Figure 10. The proposed role of the UPR pathway as core stress response to various stresses in C. neoformans.. From: Unique Evolution of the UPR Pathway with a Novel bZIP Transcription Factor, Hxl1, for Controlling Pathogenicity of Cryptococcus neoformans.

The Ire1-Hxl1-mediated UPR system plays important roles in ER quality control and is essential during growth and cell survival for cryptococcal disease. Notably, Ire1 has a broad range of physiological roles in C. neoformans not only in Hxl1-dependent but also Hxl1-independent manners. Solid arrow indicates direct involvement of the UPR pathway (e.g., ER stress) and dotted arrow indicates the direct or indirect activation of the UPR pathway via potential cross-talk with other signaling pathways.
