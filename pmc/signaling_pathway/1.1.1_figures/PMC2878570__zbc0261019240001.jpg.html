FIGURE 1. From: Receptor-independent Ambient pH Signaling by Ubiquitin Attachment to Fungal Arrestin-like PalF.

PalH dependence of PacC processing. A, sketch of the A. nidulans pH signaling pathway. Upon alkaline pH stimulation, the pal pathway (PalH, PalF, PalI, PalC, PalA, and PalB) mediates conversion of PacC72 (i.e. 72 kDa) into the committed 53-kDa intermediate PacC53, which is further processed to 27-kDa PacC27. B, Western analysis of Myc3-PacC processing in wild-type and palH− strains. Cells were cultured at pH 4.2 (H+) and shifted to pH 8.2 (OH−) for the indicated times. Actin levels were used as loading control.
