Figure 8. From: Tumor Suppressor PTEN Inhibits Integrin- and Growth Factor-mediated Mitogen-activated Protein (MAP) Kinase Signaling Pathways .

Working model of the PTEN regulatory network. Integrins and growth factors can separately stimulate the Ras/ERK/MAP kinase pathway or function collaboratively. Integrin receptor engagement with FN stimulates FAK, Shc and c-Src, and they activate the pathway as described (Schlaepfer et al., 1998). Growth factors can activate the pathway through autophosphorylation of growth factor receptors. PTEN reduces Shc phosphorylation and its interaction with Grb2, as well as the downstream Ras/ ERK common MAP kinase pathway for integrin- and growth factorâ€“mediated signaling, and then affects cell spreading and focal contact formation. Activated MEK1 can partially reverse the latter effects of PTEN.
