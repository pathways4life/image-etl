Fig. 4. From: Apolipoprotein E reduces food intake via PI3K/Akt signaling pathway in the hypothalamus.

ApoE induced the activation of PI3K/Akt signaling pathway in rat hypothalamus. 4-h fasted rats received icv LY294002 (1 nmol) or vehicle prior to dark. Sixty min later, the rats were treated icv with apoE (4 μg) or saline. After 30 min, hypothalami were microdissected and the protein extracted. Proteins were analyzed using Western blot with phospho-specific anti-pAkt antibody or antibody that recognizes the total Akt protein as loading control. Pretreatment with LY294002 (LY) attenuated apoE-induced Akt phosphorylation in the hypothalamus. A, representative immunoblots of pAkt and total Akt; B, quantitative analysis. Data represent mean ± SEM; n = 6–7, *P <0.05.
