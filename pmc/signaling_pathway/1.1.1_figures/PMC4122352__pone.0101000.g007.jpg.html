Figure 7. Enhanced BMPRIA signaling activates Smad-dependent pathway but inhibits JNK signaling pathway in the condylar cartilage.. From: BMPRIA Mediated Signaling Is Essential for Temporomandibular Joint Development in Mice.

Immunohistochemistry shows absent pSmad1/5, p-p38, and pERK, but the presence of pJNK in the E14.5 control condyle (A, C, E, G), and the presence of pSmad1/5, but absent pJNK as well as p-p38 and pERK in the transgenic condylar cartilage (B, D, F, H). Abbreviation: C, condylar cartilage; G, glenoid fossa; M, Meckel's cartilage. Scale bar = 100 µm.
