Figure 6. From: Essential Function for PDLIM2 in Cell Polarization in Three-Dimensional Cultures by Feedback Regulation of the ?1-Integrin-RhoA Signaling Axis.

PDLIM2 has an essential function in maintaining breast epithelial cell polarity at the level of feedback signaling through the β1-integrin–FAK–RhoA axis. Suppression of PDLIM2 expression prevents negative feedback control of FAK-ROCK-RhoA signaling downstream of β1-integrin resulting in hyperactivation of the pathway causing dysregulation of polarization, proliferation/apoptosis, cell-cell and cell-matrix adhesion, and loss of normal phenotype of breast epithelial cells.
