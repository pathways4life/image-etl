Figure 6. Postnatal deletion of plexin-D1 perturbs spine density and mEPSC frequency in direct but not indirect pathway MSNs. From: Semaphorin 3E-Plexin-D1 signaling controls pathway-specific synapse formation in the striatum.

(a) AAV encoding Cre-mCherry was injected into the striatum of P4–5 Drd2-GFP;plexin-D1f/f mice or their littermate controls (Drd2-GFP;plexin-D1f/+). (b) Brain slices were cut 2–3 weeks after virus injection and mCherry fluorescence was used to confirm Cre expression (right). L: lateral, A: anterior. i.c.: internal capsule. (c) Image of a brain slice from a Drd2-GFP;plexin-D1f/f mouse 2 weeks after AAV virus injection demonstrating sparse Cre expression (red) in both GFP positive (green) and negative neurons. (d) mEPSCs recorded from Cre+ direct and indirect pathway MSNs from plexin-D1f/f mice and plexin-D1f/+ control mice. (e–f) Summary of changes in mEPSC amplitude (left) and frequency (right) in direct (e) and indirect (f) pathway MSNs. (*P<0.05; Mann-Whitney). (g) Images of dendritic segments of Cre-expressing direct and indirect pathway MSNs in mice with one (top) or two (bottom) copies of the conditional plexin-D1 allele. The Cre+ direct pathway MSNs (red in c) in plexin-D1f/f animals show decreased spine density compared to those in virus-injected littermate controls (Cre+; plexin-D1f/+). In contrast, there is no detectable difference in spine density between Cre+ indirect pathway MSNs (yellow in c) in plexin-D1f/f and plexin-D1f/+ littermate control mice. (h) Summary of changes in direct and indirect pathway MSNs showing a significant difference in spine density between Cre+ plexin-D1f/f direct pathway MSNs and Cre+ plexin-D1f/+ heterozygous controls (*P<0.05; Mann-Whitney). Error bars denote s.e.m.
