Fig. 1. From: Discovery of Chemical Modulators of a Conserved Translational Control Pathway by Parallel Screening in Yeast.

Signaling to GCN4 via eIF2B. (Left) In wild-type cells amino acids (aa) repress kinase Gcn2p, maintaining low eIF2 phosphorylation (eIF2Î±P) and active eIF2B. This represses GCN4 translation. (Middle) Starvation for one or more aa activates Gcn2p, inhibiting eIF2B and activating translation of GCN4 and transcription of its target genes. (Right) Mutations in eIF2B (eIF2BM) override this signaling pathway and activate GCN4 constitutively.
