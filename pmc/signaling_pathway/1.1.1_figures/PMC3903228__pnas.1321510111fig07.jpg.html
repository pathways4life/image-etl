Consequences of coordinate activation of RHOA and ROCK1 by HIFs. Schematic diagram of hypoxia-induced and HIF-regulated RhoA/ROCK1 expression, which inhibits MYPT activity and increases MLC phosphorylation, leading to actin polymerization, cell motility, actin-myosin contractility, and the formation of focal adhesions which promote FAK activation. The pathways converge to promote cell motility, an essential step required for invasion and metastasis.
