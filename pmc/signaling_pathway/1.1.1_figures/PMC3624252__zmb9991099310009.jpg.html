Fig 9. From: Gab2 Phosphorylation by RSK Inhibits Shp2 Recruitment and Cell Motility.

Schematic representation of Gab2 signaling and its negative regulation by RSK. While Gab2 phosphorylation at tyrosine residues promotes Shp2 recruitment and activation of the Ras/MAPK pathway, RSK-mediated phosphorylation of Gab2 on Ser/Thr residues inhibits Shp2 binding and Shp2-dependent signaling. RSK-mediated phosphorylation of Gab2 likely plays a role in tightly regulating Gab2-dependent biological functions, such as the regulation of cell motility.
