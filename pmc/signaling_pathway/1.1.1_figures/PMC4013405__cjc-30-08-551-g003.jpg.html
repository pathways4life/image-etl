Figure 3.nAChR-mediated signaling in lung squamous cell carcinomas and adenocarcinomas.. From: Lung carcinoma signaling pathways activated by smoking.

nAChR-mediated mitogen-activated protein kinase (MAPK) activation in squamous cell carcinoma and adenocarcinoma is mediated by the scaffolding protein Î²-arrestin, which binds to and recruits Src to the receptors and stimulates the Raf/MAPK pathway. MAPK activation functions with the PI3K/Akt and the EGFR/Raf/MAPK signaling pathways, both activated by Ca2+ influx through nAChRs, to induce phosphorylation and activation of transcription factors, further regulate downstream gene transcription and protein expression, provoke cell proliferation, anti-apoptosis, tumor invasion, and therapy resistance.
