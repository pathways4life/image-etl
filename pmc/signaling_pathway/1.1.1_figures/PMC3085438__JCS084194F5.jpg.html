Fig. 5. From: CCN6 (WISP3) decreases ZEB1-mediated EMT and invasion by attenuation of IGF-1 receptor signaling in breast cancer.

Working model for the role of CCN6 in breast cancer invasion. Our data support the hypothesis that CCN6 regulates the levels and activity of IGF-1R signaling pathway in mammary epithelial cells. Low levels of CCN6, as found in aggressive invasive carcinomas of the breast, result in activation of IGF-1R signaling and increased ZEB1 levels and function.
