Figure 7. Go signaling downstream of the neuropeptide receptor EGL-6 is especially sensitive to irk-1 mutation. From: IRK-1 potassium channels mediate peptidergic inhibition of C. elegans serotonin neurons via a Go signaling pathway.

(A) The egg-laying defect of egl-6(gf) mutants as measured by the abnormally late developmental stages of newly laid eggs was strongly suppressed by irk-1 deletion.(B) The egg-laying defect of egl-10 mutants, which have increased Go signaling caused by loss of an RGS protein that inhibits GoÎ±, was only partly suppressed by irk-1 deletion.(C) The egg-laying defect of animals carrying a gain-of-function mutation in EGL-47, another Go-coupled receptor that inhibits HSNs, was not suppressed by irk-1 deletion.(D) A model for the function of IRK-1 channels in the regulation of HSNs by Go signaling.
