Fig. 9. From: A Golgi fragmentation pathway in neurodegeneration.

Crosstalk between subcellular organelles during neuronal cell death. Organelles sense damage to neurons and integrate apoptotic signaling. Each organelle can activate specific signal transduction pathways to promote or prevent cell death. Data presented here show that inhibition of the Golgi-mediated cell death pathway only delays apoptosis after NMDA or SNOC insult because other death pathways (e.g., mitochondrial) can intervene. In contrast, interruption of the Golgi pathway produces a more long-lasting inhibition of thapsigargin-initiated damage. Note that the ER can also directly communicate with mitochondria in apoptotic signaling using Ca2+ as a messenger (Breckenridge et al., 2003; Scorrano, 2003; Scorrano et al., 2003).
