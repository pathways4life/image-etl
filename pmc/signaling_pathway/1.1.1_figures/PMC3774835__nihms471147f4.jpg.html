Figure 4. mTORC1 is activated by ERK2/RSK1 signaling to TSC2 in LEC.219 but not in BEC.219. From: A unique herpesviral transcriptional program in KSHV-infected lymphatic endothelial cells leads to mTORC1 activation and rapamycin sensitivity.

The phosphorylation status of relevant signaling pathways was determined by immunoblotting with indicated antibodies. (A) Western blots of the substrates of mTORC1 signaling, p70 S6K (that phosphorylates S6) and 4EBP1, in mock and stably infected (inf) LEC and BEC were performed. (B) The MAPK pathway (MEK/ERK/RSK) and PI3K/AKT pathway converge at TSC2 to regulate mTORC1 signaling. (C) PI3K/AKT signaling to TSC2 T1462 shows no contribution to the mTORC1 activation in LEC.219. (D) ERK2/RSK1 signals to TSC2 S1798 in LEC.219 without any contribution from upstream MEK1/2. See also Figure S3.
