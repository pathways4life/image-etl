Fig. 7. From: Epidermal growth factor receptor expression and signaling are essential in glutamine's cytoprotective mechanism in heat-stressed intestinal epithelial-6 cells.

Proposed working model. GLN is protective in the intestine by preventing EGFR degradation after HS injury, as well as by activating the protective EGFR signaling pathway. GLN phosphorylates ERK1/2 and inactivates p38MAPK via EGFR tyrosine kinase signaling to prevent apoptosis. However, GLN is protective by activating the PI3-K pathway independently from EGFR signaling.
