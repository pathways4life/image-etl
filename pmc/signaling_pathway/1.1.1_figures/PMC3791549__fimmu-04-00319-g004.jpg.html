Figure 4. From: Nucleic Acid Sensors and Type I Interferon Production in Systemic Lupus Erythematosus.

Signaling through NLR receptors in lupus. Intracellular DNA following microbial infection or phagocytosis of immune complexes can potentially trigger the assembly of NLRs. The nucleic acid-induced signaling pathway converges on the adaptor STING and the kinase TBK1, which phosphorylates IRF3 to mediate downstream signaling events leading to transcriptional induction of type 1 IFN and other inflammatory cytokines.
