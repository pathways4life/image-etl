Figure 5. Deletion of sema3E increases mEPSC frequency and decreases spine density in direct pathway MSNs. From: Semaphorin 3E-Plexin-D1 signaling controls pathway-specific synapse formation in the striatum.

(a) Traces showing mEPSCs recorded from direct pathway MSNs from Drd2-GFP:sema3E+/+ (top) and Drd2-GFP:sema3E−/− (bottom) mice. (b) Summary of mean mEPSC frequency (top) and amplitude (bottom) in direct pathway MSNs. (*P<0.05; Mann-Whitney). (c) Traces showing mEPSCs recorded from indirect pathway MSNs from Drd2-GFP:sema3E+/+ (top) and Drd2-GFP:sema3E−/− (bottom) mice. (d) Summary of mean mEPSC frequency (top) and amplitude (bottom) in direct pathway MSNs. (e) Images of spiny dendrites of direct (top) and indirect (bottom) pathway MSNs in wildtype (left) and sema3E−/− (right) mice. (f) Summary of spine densities in direct and indirect pathway MSNs of the indicated genotypes. (*P<0.05; Mann-Whitney). Error bars denote s.e.m.
