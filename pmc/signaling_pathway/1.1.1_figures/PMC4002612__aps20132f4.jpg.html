Figure 4. From: Characterization of a novel curcumin analog P1 as potent inhibitor of the NF-?B signaling pathway with distinct mechanisms.

Effects of P1 on the inhibition of the NF-κB signaling pathway. (A) EMSA analysis of NF-κB DNA-binding activity in vitro. (B) Immunofluorescence analysis of p65 nuclear translocation in HeLa cells. Cells were pretreated with DMSO or P1 for 6 h and then stimulated with or without TNF-α. (C) HeLa cells were pretreated with P1 for 6 h and then stimulated with TNF-α for 30 min. Nuclear proteins were prepared for Western blot analysis. (D) Western blot analysis of the NF-κB signaling pathway in HeLa cells after treatment with P1 for 6 h. (E) The effect of P1 on IKK-β activity in vitro. (F) The effect of P1 on TNF-α-induced p38 activation after treatment for 6 h.
