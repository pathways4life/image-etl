Figure 2. From: The Role of microRNA in Gastric Malignancy.

Signaling cascades that regulate the intrinsic and extrinsic pathways of apoptosis. Receptor tyrosine kinase detects survival stimuli such as growth factors and induces phosphoinositide 3-kinase (PI3K)/Akt signaling cascades that ultimately result in the inhibition of the pro-apoptotic protein, Bad. Pro-apoptotic and anti-apoptotic proteins govern the intrinsic cell death pathway, which results in the release of cytochrome c from the mitochondria and induction of the caspase cascade. Signaling through death receptors initiates the extrinsic pathway of apoptosis.
