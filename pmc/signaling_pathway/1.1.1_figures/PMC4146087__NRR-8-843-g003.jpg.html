Regulation by zinc of the extracellular signal-regulated kinase (ERK) 1/2 signaling cascade.Zinc can activate the ERK1/2 signaling pathway at various levels1: (1) by inhibiting phosphatases that are involved in ERK1/2 dephosphorylation, and inhibits kinases that indirectly regulate the phosphorylation levels of receptors that subsequently activate the ERK cascade; (2) by directly binding to and activating receptors (e.g., G protein-coupled receptor 39) which have ERK1/2 as a downstream signaling cascade; (3) by activating MMPs, which subsequently cleave and activate substrates (e.g., pro-BDNF) releasing ligands (e.g., BDNF) that activate receptors upstream from the ERK1/2 cascade.MEK: Mitogen-activated protein kinase/ERK kinase; MMP: matrix metalloproteinases; BDNF: brain-derived neurotrophic factor.
