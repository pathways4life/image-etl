Fig. 5. From: Growth compensatory role of sulindac sulfide-induced thrombospondin-1 linked with ERK1/2 and RhoA GTPase signaling pathways.

Proposed contribution of TSP-1 linked with ERK1/2 and RhoA signaling pathway in NSAID-treated cells. Sulindac sulfide usually causes cellular growth suppression or cytotoxicity. As the compensatory mechanism, it also induces TSP-1 expression via ERK1/2 signaling pathway. Moreover, activated RhoA signals can enhance TSP-1 induction levels. All of the compensatory signals elevated cellular viability under the cytotoxic NSAID action.
