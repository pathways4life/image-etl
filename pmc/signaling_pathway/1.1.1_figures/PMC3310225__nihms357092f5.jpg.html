Figure 5. Modulation of the PKA pathway identified PKA-2 as a regulator of tubule morphogenesis. From: A Protein Kinase A and Wnt-dependent network regulating an intermediate stage in epithelial tubulogenesis during kidney development.

Spinal cord-induced MM was treated with PKA pathway signaling modulators and observed for defects in tubule morphogenesis. (A) Activation of PKA by stimulation of cAMP production (forskolin) or by adding cAMP analogues (dibutyryl-cAMP) prevented tubular morphogenesis. Activation of PKA-1 (8-PIP-cAMP and 8-HA-cAMPS) and Epac (8-CPT-2-O-Me-cAMP) did not inhibit tubulogenesis while activation of PKA-2 (6-MBC-cAMP and Sp-5,6-DCl-cBIMPS) prevented tubule formation.
