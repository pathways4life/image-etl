Figure 4. Deletion of plexin-D1 slightly decreases spine density in direct pathway MSNs. From: Semaphorin 3E-Plexin-D1 signaling controls pathway-specific synapse formation in the striatum.

(a) 2PLSM image of a brain slice from a Drd2-GFP;Drd1a-Cre;plexin-D1f/+ mouse showing GFP-expressing indirect pathway MSNs (green) and one MSN that was filled with Alexa Fluor-594 (red) through the whole-cell recording pipette. (b) High magnification images of spiny dendritic segments of direct (left) and indirect (right) pathway MSNs in mice that have selectively lost one (top) or both (bottom) copies of plexin-D1 in MSNs of each pathway. Direct pathway MSNs in Drd2-GFP;Drd1a-Cre;plexin-D1f/f mice show decreased spine density compared to their littermate controls. In contrast, indirect pathway MSNs in Drd2-specific plexin-D1 KO mice (Drd2-GFP;Drd2-Cre;plexin-D1f/f) show no difference in spine density compared to their littermate controls (Drd2-GFP;Drd2-Cre;plexin-D1f/+) (c) Summary of spine densities in direct and indirect pathway MSNs of the indicated genotypes. There is a small but significant loss of spines in direct pathway MSNs in Drd1a-Cre plexin-D1 conditional KO mice compared to Creâˆ’ (fl/fl Ctrl) and Cre+ (Het Ctrl) control mice (*P<0.05; Mann-Whitney). Error bars denote s.e.m.
