FIG. 6. From: G Protein-Coupled Receptor Kinase 2, With ?-Arrestin 2, Impairs Insulin-Induced Akt/Endothelial Nitric Oxide Synthase Signaling in ob/ob Mouse Aorta.

Proposed model of GRK2-mediated inhibition of Akt/eNOS pathway in diabetes. In normal aortas, binding of insulin to IR activates Akt/eNOS pathway via β-arrestin 2 (βarr2) activation, and GRK2 is not translocated. In diabetes, however, GRK2, which is present in increased quantities, binds to Akt (and βarr2 is not activated). This leads to reductions of Akt/eNOS signaling and NO production in the membrane.
