Figure 3. From: IBP-mediated suppression of autophagy promotes growth and metastasis of breast cancer cells via activating mTORC2/Akt/FOXO3a signaling pathway.

IBP-mediated inhibition of autophagy is dependent on the mTORC2 signaling pathway in breast cancer cells. (a and b) MDA-MB-231 and MDA-MB-468 cells with different levels of IBP were treated without or with LY294002 (25 μm), rapamycin (10nm), OSI-027 (10 μm). Immunoblotting detected the levels of autophagy. (c and d) Cells were incubated with either rapamycin (10 nm) or OSI-027 (10 μm) or combined the two for 2 h. Immunoblotting detected the level of p-mTOR Ser2481, p-Akt Ser473, p-FOXO3a Thr32 and LC3-II
