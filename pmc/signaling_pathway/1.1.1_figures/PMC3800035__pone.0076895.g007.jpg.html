Figure 7. Schematic representation of differential utilization of co-receptors and G-proteins for β-catenin-dependent and β-catenin-independent signaling pathways.. From: Heterotrimeric G-Protein, Gα16, Is a Critical Downstream Effector of Non-Canonical Wnt Signaling and a Potent Inhibitor of Transformed Cell Growth in Non Small Cell Lung Cancer.

Based on our current understanding of Wnt-sensitive pathways, we propose that Wnt binding to Fzd and co-receptor LRP5/6, and mediated by Gαo, leads to activation of β-catenin-dependent gene transcription. While, Wnt binding to Fzd and co-receptor ROR1/2, and mediated by Gα16, leads to the activation of β-catenin-independent signaling pathway, mainly via activation of ERK5 and PPARγ.
