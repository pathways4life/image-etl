Figure 1. IFN signaling and the regulatory role of ubiquitination. From: Ubiquitination-mediated regulation of interferon responses.

Upon engaging a cognate receptor complex (IFNAR1-IFNAR2 for Type 1 IFN, IFNGR1-IFNGR2 for Type 2 IFN or IL28RA-IL10R2 for Type 3 IFN) IFN activate receptor-associated JAKs. A resulting tyrosine phosphorylation of STAT proteins leads to the formation of transcriptionally active complexes (STAT1 homodimers for all types and additional STAT1-STAT2-IRF9 complexes for Types 1/3 IFN) and ensuing induction of ISGs. Cellular (Î²-Trcp, SOCS, SLIM) and diverse viral E3 ubiquitin ligases facilitate the ubiquitination of various mediators of IFN signaling pathway to limit the extent and duration of this signaling.
