FIGURE 8. From: HIV-1 Glycoprotein 41 Ectodomain Induces Activation of the CD74 Protein-mediated Extracellular Signal-regulated Kinase/Mitogen-activated Protein Kinase Pathway to Enhance Viral Infection.

Model of enhancement of HIV-1 infection as a result of HIV-1 gp41 binding to CD74 and subsequent activation of the ERK/MAPK signal pathway. a, gp41, which is exposed during membrane fusion, binds CD74 and activates the ERK/MAPK pathway. b, gp41 existing on defective virions directly binds CD74 and activates the ERK/MAPK pathway. c, degraded gp41 fragments containing loop sequence (like peptide 6358) bind CD74 to activate the ERK/MAPK pathway and enhance HIV-1 infection. The ERK/MAPK downstream signaling pathway has been described elsewhere (46, 51).
