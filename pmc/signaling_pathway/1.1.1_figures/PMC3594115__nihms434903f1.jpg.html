Figure 1. Pathway-specific impairment of insulin signaling pathway and endothelial dysfunction. From: Role of Insulin Resistance in Endothelial Dysfunction.

PI 3-kinase branch of insulin signaling regulates NO production and vasodilation in vascular endothelium. MAP-kinase branch of insulin signaling controls secretion of endothelin-1 (ET-1) and adhesion molecule expression in vascular endothelium. Glucotoxicity, lipotoxicity, and various cytokines activate signaling molecules that inhibit PI3K/Akt signaling. eNOS, endothelial nitric oxide synthase; IRS, insulin receptor substrate; MEK, MAPK kinase; PDK, phosphoinositide-dependent protein kinase; PKC, protein kinase C; IRS, insulin receptor substrate; ERK, extracellular signal-regulated kinase; JNK, C-Jun N-terminal kinase; p70S6K, p70 ribosomal S6 kinase; AP-1, activator protein-1; NO, nitric oxide; and ET-1, endothelin-1.
