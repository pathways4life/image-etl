Fig. 6. An illustration showing the modulation of the BRAF/MEK/ERK/RSK2 signaling pathway by silybin in SK-MEL-5 cells. From: Direct targeting of MEK1/2 and RSK2 by silybin induces cell cycle arrest and inhibits melanoma cell growth.

In melanoma, the mutation of BRAF (V600E) constitutively activates the down–stream kinase MEK1/2, which phosphorylates ERK2. Phosphorylated ERK2 activates RSK2, which transmits a signal downstream to transcription factors, such as AP-1, NF-κB and STAT3, and induces their transcriptional activity to express cyclin D1. Silybin inhibits this oncogenic signaling pathway by direct regulation of MEK1/2 and RSK2.
