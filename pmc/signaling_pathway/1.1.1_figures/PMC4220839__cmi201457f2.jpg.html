Figure 2. From: A new cytosolic DNA-recognition pathway for DNA-induced inflammatory responses.

Cytosolic DNA recognition pathway for IL-1β production. Upon recognition of DNA, AIM2 interacts with ASC, a common adaptor of the inflammasome, leading to the assembly of an inflammasome and the cleavage of caspase-1, which induces the processing of pro-IL-1β and the secretion of IL-1β. Roth et al.4 demonstrated a new cytosolic DNA sensing pathway that was able to induce IL-1β. The presence of DNA or a DNA virus in the cytosol induces the formation of dsDNA–Rad50–CARD9 signaling complexes, which control NF-κB activation and the transcription of the gene encoding pro-IL-1β. AIM2, absent in melanoma 2; CARD9, caspase recruitment domain-containing protein 9.
