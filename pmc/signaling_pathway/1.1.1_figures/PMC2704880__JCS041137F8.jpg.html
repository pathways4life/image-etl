Fig. 8. From: The yeast endocytic protein Epsin 2 functions in a cell-division signaling pathway.

Model for yeast epsin function in cell polarity and division. Yeast epsins, Ent1 and Ent2, bind Cdc42 GAPs Rga1 and Rga2 to activate Cdc42 signaling towards cell polarity (Gic1/2-dependent) establishment pathways (Aguilar et al., 2006). From this study we propose that epsins (particularly Ent2) affect septin organization pathways by interacting with another GAP, Bem3, which is also involved in septin ring formation. This novel septin-regulatory circuit would be redundant with another pathway (X), yet to be identified.
