FIGURE 1. From: Multiscale Models of Cell Signaling.

Multiple subcellular scales of signal transduction. Signaling molecules undergo posttranslational modifications to modulate activity within a pathway. Multiple pathways interconnect to form a signaling network, which collectively integrates external cues to change cellular behavior. The multiscale properties of signal transduction can give rise to surprising cellular responses when signaling is perturbed, as with the B-Raf inhibitor PLX4032.34,70 Models seek to gain additional biological insight by bridging adjacent scales.
