Fig. 9. Scheme for the cell signaling response to ischemia. From: CAVEOLAE ARE AN ESSENTIAL COMPONENT OF THE PATHWAY FOR ENDOTHELIAL CELL SIGNALING ASSOCIATED WITH ABRUPT REDUCTION OF SHEAR STRESS.

Altered shear stress is “sensed” by caveolae resulting in KATP channel closure and cell membrane depolarization. Subsequent activation of NADPH oxidase (NOX2) and downstream signaling pathways results in cell proliferation which may be a compensatory mechanism to restore perfusion.
