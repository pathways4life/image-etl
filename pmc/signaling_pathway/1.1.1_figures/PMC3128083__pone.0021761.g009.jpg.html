Figure 9. Schematic diagram showing RIG-I signaling pathway leading to production of proinflammatory cyto/chemokines from neurons.. From: RIG-I Mediates Innate Immune Response in Mouse Neurons Following Japanese Encephalitis Virus Infection.

Detection of viral ss/dsRNA by RIG-I activates a cascade of changes that leads to activation of P38MAPK and NFκB. As a result of such activation, proinflammatory cyto/chemokine gene transcription is enhanced in neurons that ultimately contribute to the inflammatory milieu in the brain, following JEV infection. Ablation of RIG-I using antisense morpholino oligonucleotide results in negative modulation of this pathway. ↑-upregulation; ↓-downregulation; ⊢ -ablation.
