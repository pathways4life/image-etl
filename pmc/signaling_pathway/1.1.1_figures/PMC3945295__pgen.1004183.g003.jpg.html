Figure 3. Network connectivity of Sks1p kinase signaling.. From: The Yeast Sks1p Kinase Signaling Network Regulates Pseudohyphal Growth and Glucose Response.

A) Network connectivity map constructed by expanding connections involving Sks1p-dependent phosphoproteins through genetic and physical interactions from KEGG, BioGrid, and GeneMania. Clusters of proteins enriched for the indicated KEGG signaling pathways are shown. Numbered proteins identify Sks1p and a subset of proteins exhibiting Sks1p-dependent phosphorylation by mass spectrometry (shaded red) within the context of the larger connected network. As indicated, the KEGG glycolysis/gluconeogenesis pathway was enriched in the subset of Sks1p-dependent phosphoproteins identified by mass spectrometry. Proteins within the network clustered around B) the KEGG glycolysis/gluconeogenesis pathway and C) the KEGG cell cycle and meiosis pathways are highlighted.
