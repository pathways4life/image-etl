Figure 3. Role of GRP78 and its isoform GRP78va in regulating UPR signaling pathway and cell survival. From: Beyond the endoplasmic reticulum: atypical GRP78 in cell viability, signaling and therapeutic targeting.

ER stress induces Grp78 transcription, resulting in upregulation of GRP78 and GRP78va, the latter is generated by alternative splicing. In the ER lumen, GRP78, in association with P58IPK and other co-chaperones, enhances protein folding and degradation of misfolded proteins (ERAD). Release of GRP78 from PERK, IRE1 and ATF6 induces the UPR signaling. PERK activation leads to eIF2Î± phosphorylation and translation attenuation, contributing to cell survival. In the cytosol, GRP78va enforces PERK signaling by inactivation of the cytosolic P58IPK.
