Fig. 4. From: Inhibition of cancer cell invasion and metastasis by genistein.

Genistein inhibits critical pathways in cancer invasion. Our group has demonstrated that genistein can specifically target MEK4. This inhibition results in inactivation of the MEK4 pathway, decreased MMP-2 production, and decreased cell invasion. Genistein also activates Smad1, which is activated by the endoglin signaling pathway, and causes decreased cell invasion. Additionally, genistein inhibits FAK activation, resulting in increased cell adhesion.
