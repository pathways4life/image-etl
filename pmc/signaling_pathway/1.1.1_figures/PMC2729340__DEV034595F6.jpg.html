Fig. 6. From: Somatic cAMP signaling regulates MSP-dependent oocyte growth and meiotic maturation in C. elegans.

A model for the control of oocyte meiotic maturation by Gαs-adenylate cyclase signaling in the gonadal sheath cells. MSP is proposed to trigger Gαs-adenylate cyclase signaling resulting in PKA activation. Based on genetic epistasis analysis, Gαs-adenylate cyclase signaling is proposed to antagonize at least two pathways that inhibit meiotic maturation, one involving the germline innexins INX-14 and INX-22, and an uncharacterized pathway defined by mutations (X?) that suppress acy-4(lf) (S.K., J.A.G. and D.G., unpublished).
