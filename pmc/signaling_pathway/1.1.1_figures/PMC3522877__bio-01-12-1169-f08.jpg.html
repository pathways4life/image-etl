Fig. 8.A schematic representation of anti-motility versus anti-growth signaling by TGFβ3 in two human skin cell types critical for wound healing.. From: The anti-motility signaling mechanism of TGF?3 that controls cell traffic during skin wound healing.

While TGFβ3 blocks proliferation of both HDFs and HKCs, it selectively inhibits migration of HDFs, but not HKCs, due to 7 to 10 fold higher expression of TβRII (Bandyopadhyay et al., 2006). TGFβ3 uses TβRII/TβRI/Smads2/3/Smad4/PKA pathway to transmit its anti-motility signal. The anti-proliferation signaling by TGFβ is beyond the scope of this study. The numbers in “n  = ” are arbitrary to reflect the differences in TβR expression in fold between HDFs and HKCs.
