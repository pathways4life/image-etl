Figure 2. OGT and O-GlcNAc Regulate Insulin Signaling. From: O-GlcNAc Signaling: A Metabolic Link Between Diabetes and Cancer?.

During prolonged insulin signaling O-GlcNAcylation of key molecules have an affect on the attenuation of the insulin signaling cascade. As with any signaling cascade, there must be a mechanism in place to down-regulate the cascade after a prolonged period of activation. O-GlcNAcylation of key molecules can achieve this via the translocation of OGT in response to insulin.
